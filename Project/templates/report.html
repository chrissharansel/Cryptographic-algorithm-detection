Potential Vulnerabilities

- ECB mode has known weaknesses because it doesn't hide data patterns well.

- If a weak key is chosen, even AES-256 can be vulnerable to brute-force attacks.

Suggestions for Improvement

- Switch from ECB to CBC or GCM mode for enhanced security and to hide data patterns.

- Use a longer key size, like 256-bit, to increase the difficulty of brute-force attacks.

- Implement key rotation to regularly change encryption keys and reduce the impact of key compromise.

Extra Layer of Security

- Consider adding RSA or ECC on top of AES for hybrid encryption, ensuring both symmetric and asymmetric cryptography are used.

- For sensitive applications, combining AES with ChaCha20 can provide both speed and security.

- Apply digital signatures to authenticate data integrity and ensure that no tampering has occurred.
